Coop Blog 1

Introduction

For the first 4 months of my 8-month co-op, I worked as a Student Consultant at the University of Guelph. In this blog, I aim to share the experiences I've gained, the skills I've developed, and the challenges I've faced so far in this role. Whether you're interested in IT support, exploring co-op opportunities, or just curious about what it's like working in this position, I aim to provide valuable insights. My goal is to emphasize the importance of technical knowledge, effective communication, and teamwork in a university environment.

Information about the Employer

CCS at the University of Guelph is the tech backbone of the campus! From keeping Wi-Fi running smoothly to supporting Gryph Mail and Office 365, they ensure everything tech-related works like a charm. Located right on campus, CCS is where IT magic happens, helping students, staff, and faculty stay connected and secure. Shoutout to my amazing supervisor, Jill Hogg, for making this co-op experience even more incredible!

Coop Goals

For my co-op term, my goals and learning outcomes included:


  • Strengthen My Communication Skills

    Action Plan: Actively participating in daily interactions with clients and team members, practice clear and concise communication, and seek feedback to refine my verbal and written skills. Measure of Success: Improvement will be measured by positive client and supervisor feedback, increased clarity in written support tickets, and reduced follow-up questions.


  • Enhance Problem-Solving Abilities

    Action Plan: I will tackle a variety of troubleshooting scenarios, document challenges and solutions in a problem-solving journal, and seek feedback on my problem-solving approach from peers and supervisors. Measure of Success: Success will be assessed by the number and complexity of issues resolved, and noticeable improvements in problem-solving effectiveness.


  • Gain Comprehensive IT Support Knowledge

    Action Plan: I will familiarize myself with common IT support tasks and tools (LDAP and AD), shadow experienced IT staff, and engage in hands-on practice with different support systems and software.

    Measure of Success: I will track the number and variety of issues resolved, self-assess my performance regularly, and aim for increased successful resolutions over time.

  • Improve Professional and Ethical Behavior in Teamwork

    Action Plan: I will actively engage with my team during regular tasks, offering assistance and maintaining open communication to ensure smooth collaboration. Measure of Success: Success will be measured by positive feedback from team members and improved efficiency in handling shared responsibilities.
